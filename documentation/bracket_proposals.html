About the format

68 teams, one Sunday. On Selection Sunday (mid-March), the champions of the 31 conferences receive an automatic bid, and a selection committee awards 37 at-large bids to the best remaining teams.

East, West, South and Midwest are not conferences — they are the four quarters of the bracket. To fill them, the committee first ranks all 68 teams from 1 to 68 (the "seed list"), then deals them out following an S-curve: the top four teams become the four No. 1 seeds (one per region), the next four the No. 2 seeds, and so on — snaking the order at each pass so the four regions end up as balanced as possible (on paper, the overall No. 1 gets the weakest No. 2, etc.).

Geography and separation rules then adjust the placement: top seeds play their opening games as close to campus as possible (first-round sites are grouped in geographic "pods"), teams from the same conference are kept apart until the deep rounds, and regular-season rematches are avoided early.

First Four. The four weakest automatic qualifiers and the four last at-large teams play four play-in games (in Dayton, Ohio) to trim the field from 68 to 64.

Then it's single elimination. 1st Round → 2nd Round → Sweet 16 → Elite 8 crowns each region's champion; the four region champions meet at the Final Four — two semifinals and the National Championship, one weekend, one city. Lose once and you're out: that's the madness.
